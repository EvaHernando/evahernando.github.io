Evaluating User perception

Sept 2012- Nov 2012

Client confidential. Experientia.

Context


During my time at Experientia, I worked on a research project in the pharmaceutical sector. The purpose of this research was to test the perception of a pharmaceutical product concept by both users and sellers, and to learn about the current understanding of the product category. Experientia designed and carried out focus groups with both types of participants in Italy, France and Spain. I worked in the development of the study guides of the focus groups, the analysis of findings and the presentation to the client. I was also responsible for going to the field in Spain, bringing back results and analyzing them together with the other location coordinators.









What we did


The project aimed to gauge decision influencersâ€™ level of understanding of the product category; learn preferences for the product category features; uncover marketing communication strategies to signal the product benefits; and learn if the new product concept would be accepted and has potential to win over competition.



After an initial exploratory phase in the field, in which we went to do mistery shopping of the current product. We conducted 3 different focus groups sessions in each country, addressing a total of 54 consumers and 18 sellers of the current product or one of their competitors. We collected numerous findings, and formulated high-level insights and resulting recommendations. Experientia provided strategic recommendations and design guidelines for new product and packaging concepts. We also evaluated the impact during the selling experience of the different actions and suggesteddesign solutions.







Insights led to significant recommendations for alterations to the new product, due to an ambivalent response to key features of the concept. We uncovered a gap in product knowledge on the part of decision influencers and users, leading to recommendations for greater point-of-sale support and focus on purchase experience. We also identified strong preferences for packaging by users, influencing probability of purchase. Product and packaging reiteration and further testing were strongly recommended.
